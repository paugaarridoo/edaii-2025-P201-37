Aculops lycopersici
Aculops lycopersici, also known as the tomato russet mite, is a species of mite that belongs to the family Eriophyidae.
Aculops lycopersici is an important pest of tomato plants. It was first described by A. M. Massee in 1937 in [Australia](1659), but is now common around the world.
Its genome has been sequenced and is only 32.5 Mb.
