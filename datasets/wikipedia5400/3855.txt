Aristarchus's inequality
Aristarchus's inequality (after the Greek astronomer and mathematician [Aristarchus of Samos](3856); c. 310 – c. 230 BCE) is a law of trigonometry which states that if α and β are acute angles (i.e. between 0 and a right angle) and β < α then

  
    
      
        
          
            
              sin
              ⁡
              α
            
            
              sin
              ⁡
              β

            α

Ptolemy used the first of these inequalities while constructing his table of chords.
