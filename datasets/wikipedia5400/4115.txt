Alexander Bradshaw
Alexander Marian Bradshaw  (12 July 1944 â€“ 10 October 2024) was a British physicist. He was scientific director of the Max Planck Institute for Plasma Physics, from 1999 to 2008.
Bradshaw earned a PhD from University of London in 1969, and habilitation from Technical University Munich in 1974. He is also notable for his work with D. Phil Woodruff, on photoelectron diffraction.
Bradshaw died in [Berlin](1387) on 10 October 2024, at the age of 80.
