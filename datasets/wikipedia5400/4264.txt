Province of Barcelona
[Barcelona](1000) (Catalan: bəɾsəˈlonə ; Spanish: baɾθeˈlona) is a province of eastern Spain, in the center of the autonomous community of [Catalonia](2549). The province is bordered by the provinces of Tarragona, Lleida, and Girona, and by the Mediterranean Sea.  Its area is 7,726 km2 (2,983 sq mi). A total of 5,743,402 people live in the province, of whom about 29% (1,664,182) live within the administrative limits of the city of [Barcelona](1000), which itself is contained in the [Barcelona](1000) metropolitan area.

Divisions
The capital of the province is the city of [Barcelona](1000), and the provincial council is based in the Casa Serra on the Rambla de Catalunya in that city. Some other cities and towns in [Barcelona](1000) province include L'Hospitalet de Llobregat, Badalona, Cerdanyola del Vallès, Martorell, Mataró, Granollers, Sabadell, Terrassa, Sitges, Igualada, Vic, Manresa, and Berga.

Geography
The Catalan Pre-Coastal Range and Catalan Coastal Range mountains run through the Province of [Barcelona](1000). There are several notable smaller mountain ranges that are located in the province, including Montseny Massif, Serra de Collserola, and Tibidabo. Pedraforca is the tallest mountain in the province, located on the north side as part of the Pre-Pyrenees. Tibidabo is the mountain that overlooks the city of [Barcelona](1000).
The majority of the Province of [Barcelona](1000) has a Mediterranean climate on the coast and an oceanic climate inland.
